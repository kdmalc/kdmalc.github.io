<!-- Kai Malcolm -->

I am a 4th-year Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at **Rice University**, where I am a member of [Dr. Momona Yamagami's research group](https://yamagami.rice.edu/). 

**I am currently seeking full-time roles** as a Machine Learning Engineer or Research Scientist starting December 2027.

My research focuses on the intersection of **neural engineering and machine learning**, specifically developing **closed-loop, co-adaptive neural interfaces**. I am passionate about designing personalized interfaces that offer intuitive, high-bandwidth human-machine interaction. 

### Research & Expertise
My work primarily involves decoding human intent from wearable sensors like **Electromyography (EMG)** and **Inertial Measurement Units (IMU)**. To handle the inherent variability in human biological signals, I leverage advanced deep learning techniques for time-series data, including:
* **Personalization & Adaptation:** Federated learning, few-shot learning (meta-learning, transfer learning, and domain adaptation).
* **Systems:** Real-time closed-loop control and human-in-the-loop co-adaptation.

Prior to Rice, I earned my Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Mechanical Engineering from **Vanderbilt University**.
